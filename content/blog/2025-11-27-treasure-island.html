--- title: treasure island ---

Greg Wagland's version of this is great. I listened to a version on YouTube uploaded by (I believe) the man himself. He does a great Long John Silver and made the whole thing even more enjoyable than I think I would have found it to read headwise.

  • It's wild to think how many of today's pirate tropes this popularized or influenced☠️ Yo ho ho and a bottle of rum!
  • Pew was a really menacing minor villain! I was genuinely scared of him.
  • Despite the mutiny, everyone has a very strong code of honour. Jim makes promises to the pirates and desperately hopes to keep them, to his great personal risk.
  • They didn't know what caused malaria back then. The doctor is going on about the quality of the air: low, boggy air was bad. dry, mountainous air was good. Cool to see some proto-science.
  • The novel's flooded with nautical terminology. How many of these words and terms did the average British kid know back then?

I think I might be entering my audiobook era. Going for a run getting lost in a story is a delightful double-dip.

☠️ much of which is attributable to disney at this point - 1950's treasure island, 1996's muppet treasure island, and 2002's treasure planet.
