mosa lina is a platformer game where you solve randomly generated level-puzzles using fickle and oblique tools.

imagine KidPix meets Baba is You meets N+ meets nopaint.art

there are around 20 different tools in total, but each time you start the game, you get a smaller subset of the 20 to beat a suite of 10 randomly generated levels. each time you attempt a level, you get 3 tools from your subset to use. each time you die (or choose to skip), you spawn in one of the remaining unsolved levels with a new hand of 3 tools. when you beat the suite, you do it all again.

the chaos and impermenance of the game very naturally encourages experimentation. some levels feel impossible to solve with the hand you're dealt, but over time you discover interactions between tools and develop new intuitions for how to best go about beating a level (solveability is not guaranteed!)

the game is light on overarching objectives. mostly it's about thinking creatively, encouraged by the reticent, wry, and whimsical tone that comes through from the developers.

and because "figuring out the game" is the game, even playing it for 20 minutes feels like i'm playing it right, which is really nice in this world of meticulous and mechanically dense games.
